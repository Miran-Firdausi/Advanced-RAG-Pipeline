
This document is a pocket-sized edition of the Constitution of India, updated as of May 1, 2024. It incorporates all amendments up to the Constitution (One Hundred and Sixth Amendment) Act, 2023. The document includes the Preamble, the full text of the Constitution's articles organized into parts, and several appendices.



The document's preface states that it is the sixth pocket-sized edition in diglot form. Footnotes are used to indicate which Constitution Amendment Acts have made specific changes.

Appendices are included for reference and provide details on:

The Constitution (One Hundredth Amendment) Act, 2015, which concerns the exchange of territories between India and Bangladesh.

The Constitution (Application to Jammu and Kashmir) Order, 2019.

The declaration under article 370(3) of the Constitution.

The Preamble establishes India as a "SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC" and aims to secure justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity for all its citizens.

The content is organized into 22 parts, covering various aspects of the Indian state and its governance. These parts include:

Part I: The Union and its Territory.

Part II: Citizenship.

Part III: Fundamental Rights.

Part IV: Directive Principles of State Policy.

Part V: The Union.

Part VI: The States.

Parts IX, IXA, and IXB: The Panchayats, Municipalities, and Co-operative Societies, respectively.



Parts XI to XXII: These parts cover relations between the Union and States, finance, trade, services, elections, special provisions for certain classes, official language, emergency provisions, amendment procedures, and temporary provisions.





The document also details the various schedules, including the First Schedule, which lists the States and Union territories and their respective territories. Other schedules cover provisions for officials' salaries, forms of oaths, allocation of seats in the Council of States, and lists related to legislative powers, languages, and specific acts and regulations.
